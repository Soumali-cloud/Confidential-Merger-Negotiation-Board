Technical Deep Dive

Cryptographic Commitment

The foundation of proof integrity:

  • Hash Function: SHA256
  • Input:(revenue, assets, liabilities, nonce)
  • Output: 256-bit commitment hash
  • Purpose: Bind financial data
  • Security: Collision-resistant

Prevents witness modification and ensures proof authenticity.

Digital Signature

Authentication and authorization:

  • Algorithm: ECDSA
  • Curve: Prime256v1
  • Sign Data: Proof metadata
  • Verify With: Public key
  • Output: (r, s) signature pair

Certifies proof origin and prevents tampering.

All conditions evaluated before blockchain submission.

Nonce Generation

Unique identifier for each proof:

  • Input: company_id + timestamp
  • Hash: SHA256(concatenated input)
  • Truncated: First 16 bytes
  • Purpose: Prevent replay attacks
  • Uniqueness: Deterministic per proof

Ensures each proof has unique identifier.

Frequently Asked Questions

How secure are these proofs?

Extremely secure. We use SHA256 commitment hashing, ECDSA digital signatures, and nonce protection. Military-grade cryptography ensures proof authenticity and prevents tampering.

Can someone forge a proof?

No. Proofs are cryptographically signed with your company's unique ECDSA key. Any tampering invalidates the signature, making forgery impossible.

What if a company lies about their financials?

The cryptographic commitment hash binds their claimed figures. Any deviation from the saved commitment is detected during verification, exposing dishonesty.

How long does proof generation take?

Typically under 5 seconds. Real-time validation provides instant feedback. Blockchain submission may take additional time depending on network conditions.

Can I update a proof?

Yes! Generate new proofs with updated financials. Previous proofs remain on blockchain for audit trail. This allows deals to stay aligned with current company status.

What blockchain does this use?

Midnight Network - a specialized blockchain designed for privacy-preserving applications. Provides immutable, transparent records while protecting sensitive data.
